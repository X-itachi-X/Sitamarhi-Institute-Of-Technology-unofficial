  • Mandar Hills is a scenic hilly region located in the Banka district of the Indian state of Bihar.

  • It is known for its religious significance as it is believed to be the place where Lord Vishnu took the form of a tortoise (Kurma Avatar) and supported the Mandar Mountain during the churning of the cosmic ocean by the gods and demons in Hindu mythology.

  • The hills are part of the Rajgir hills and are situated about 50 kilometers southeast of the town of Bhagalpur.

  • The main temple on the Mandar Hills is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is called the Mandar Parvat Temple. It is believed to be more than 2,000 years old.

  • The annual Makar Sankranti festival attracts a large number of devotees to Mandar Hills. During this festival, people gather to worship Lord Vishnu and take a dip in the holy pond located at the foothills.

  • According to local legends, the Mandar Parvat Temple houses the footprints of Lord Vishnu, which are considered sacred by the devotees.

  • The hills also have a reference in the epic Ramayana, where it is mentioned as the place where Hanuman got hurt while carrying the Sanjeevani herb for Lord Lakshmana.
