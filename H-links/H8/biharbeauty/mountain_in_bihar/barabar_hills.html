Barabar Hills

  • Location: The Barabar Hills are located in Jehanabad district, Bihar, India. They are part of the Barabar and Nagarjuni Hills range.

  • Historical Significance: The Barabar Hills are renowned for their historical and archaeological importance. They contain some of the oldest surviving rock-cut caves in India.

  • Rock-cut Caves: The hills are home to several rock-cut caves, including the famous Lomas Rishi Cave and Sudama Cave. These caves were carved out of granite rocks during the Mauryan period (322-185 BCE).

  • Mauryan Era: The caves at Barabar Hills were commissioned by the Mauryan Emperor Ashoka and his son Dasaratha. They were used by the Ajivika sect, an ancient Indian religious group.

  • Architectural Marvel: The caves at Barabar Hills are known for their unique architectural style. The interiors of the caves were polished to a high degree and bear inscriptions and sculptures.

  • Jain Influence: Over time, the caves at Barabar Hills were later used by Jain monks, evident from Jain inscriptions found within the caves.
