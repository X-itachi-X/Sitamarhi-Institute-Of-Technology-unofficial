Kakolat Falls

  • Location: Kakolat Falls is located in Nawada district of Bihar, India. It is situated on the Kakolat Hill, about 33 kilometers away from Nawada town.

  • Natural Waterfall: Kakolat Falls is a natural waterfall formed by the Kakolat River, which originates from the Kakolat Hills. It is a picturesque waterfall that cascades down from a height of approximately 160 feet (49 meters).

  • Religious Significance: The Kakolat Falls hold religious significance among the locals. It is believed that taking a dip in the waterfall's plunge pool can wash away sins and purify the soul.

  • Scenic Beauty: The surroundings of Kakolat Falls are adorned with lush green forests and rocky hills, making it a popular tourist spot. The picturesque landscape and the roaring waterfall create a serene and captivating ambiance.

  • Accessibility: Kakolat Falls is easily accessible by road. It is well-connected to nearby cities like Nawada, Gaya, and Patna. The nearest railway station is at Nawada, and the nearest airport is Gaya Airport.

  • Local Folklore: According to local folklore, the name "Kakolat" is derived from the story of Lord Rama and Sita. It is believed that Lord Rama, during his exile, visited this place with Sita and spent some time here.

  • Nearby Attractions: Kakolat Falls is surrounded by several other tourist attractions. Some notable places to visit near Kakolat Falls include Rajgir, Pawapuri, and Nalanda, which are known for their historical and cultural significance.
