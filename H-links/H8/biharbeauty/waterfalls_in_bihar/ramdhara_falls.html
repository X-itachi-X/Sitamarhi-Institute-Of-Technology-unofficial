Ramdhara Falls

  • The Rajgir Zoo Safari or Rajgir Wildlife Safari is a Wildlife safari located in Rajgir, Bihar, India, was opened to public on 16 February 2022. The zoo safari has been developed by the Department of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Govt of Bihar at a cost of nearly ₹177 crore

  • It is a part of the Nalanda Forest Division.

  • There are various spots inside Rajgir wildlife safari that you must visit like Zipline or Glass Bridge.

  • It is made up of five mountains Ratnagiri, Vipulgiri, Vaibhargiri, Songiri, and Udaygiri.

  • The mixed deciduous forest contains ecologically significant species of flora and wildlife, and the sanctuary’s woodlands assist recharge of the groundwater aquifer and prevent siltation of rivers and streams by reducing soil erosion.

  • Species of vital importance are: Wild boar (Sus scrofa), nilgai (Boselaphus tragocamelus), chital (Axis axis), red junglefowl (Gallus gallus murghi), Indian crested porcupine (Hystrix indica), Indian hare (Lepus nigricollis), northern plains gray langur (Semnopithecus entellus), rhesus macaque (Macaca mulatta), and golden jackal (Canis aureus).

  • Some of the endangered species found are the Indian python (Python molurus) etc.
