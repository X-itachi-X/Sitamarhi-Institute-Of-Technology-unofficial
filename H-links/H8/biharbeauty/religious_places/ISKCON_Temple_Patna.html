ISKON Temple Patna

  • ISKCON Temple is also known as Sri Radha Banke Bihari Ji Temple. Three deities, Sri Gaur Nitai, Sri Radha Banke Bihari, and Sri Ram Darbar are in this temple.

  • The foundation stone of Patna ISKCON Temple ( also known as Sri-Sri Radha Banke Bihari Ji Temple ), was laid by the then Bihar Chief Minister in April 2007.

  • ISKCON is a religious organization and its main focus is on Hindu God Sri Krishna. All Altair of the three deities, Sri-Sri Gaur Nitai, Sri Sri Radha Banke Bihari Ji, and Sri-Sri Ram Darbar are there in this building.

  • It is believed to be the largest Krishna Temple in Patna and in the entire state of Bihar.

  • A number of religious activities like Yatras and important festivals are celebrated here. Every day Harinam Sankirtan is held here. The impressive and regal facade of the temple gives it an attractive look. Exquisitely carved statues of Radha and Krishna are there inside the temple. Every devotee is given food as prasad.
